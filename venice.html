VENICE

       Venice, the capital of northern Italy’s Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands 
        in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals – including the Grand Canal
        thoroughfare – lined with Renaissance and Gothic palaces. The central square, Piazza San Marco,
        contains St. Mark’s Basilica, which is tiled with Byzantine mosaics, and the Campanile bell tower
        offering views of the city’s red roofs.
